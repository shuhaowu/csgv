How to use

Explorer View

The explorer view offers a geographical way for views to find schools and get information from them. There are five views associated with the explorer view: enrollment default view, enrollment heatmap, rank default view, rank heatmap, and most improved heatmap.

The default views (enrollment and grade defaults) shows only a handful of schools when zoomed out. However, when you zoom in, the system will find schools that are less significant and put them on the screen. For example, in the enrollment default view, at default zoomlevel you only see the biggest schools in terms of population. As you zoom in, smaller schools starts appearing.

Note Red markers are for high schools and blue are for elementary/middle schools. Try clicking on them to see more information.

You can also search for schools using their names from the search bar at the top right of the screen.

Enrollment default
Shows all the school with markers. Smaller schools requires zooming in.
Enrollment heatmap
May be slow Shows all the school in the form of a heat map. This is view is best at showing where student populations are concentrated.
Rank default
Slow This view uses a circular marker to show schools and their ranks. Schools with better ranks will be bigger while schools with lower ranks will be smaller. Similarly to the default view, zooming in will show schools with lower ranks.
Rank heatmap
May be slow Shows the ranks with a heatmap. While what this map shows is speculative, one can see from this view that the ranks are not completely dependent on population.
Improved heatmap
This view shows where the top 200 schools in term of being most improved from 2010 to 2012 are concentrated.
Tabular View

The tabular view offers to segregate the data into different years and levels in a table format. This table can be sorted by columns, allowing users to find connections between data.

Note Clicking on the names of each school will bring up the same details dialog as the explorer view details dialog.

Terms and Definitions

Explorer View
School Level
The school level. This could be elementary, intermediate (middle schools), or secondary (high schools).
Change
This is the yearly trend of the school's academic achievement grade (Achievement Change), academic growth grade (Growth Change), and school grade (Score Change).
COACT
This identifies whether of not the students at a particular school are ready for college or career based on their ACT (American College Testing) scores. Specifically, these stats shows if the average student in the school have a 75% chance of earning a C or above in a corresponding college course (✓ for yes and ✗ for no).
Similar Schools
Based on the statistic of that school (either by grade or enrollment), identify schools that are similar
As Elementary, As Middle...
Many schools can span from elementary school all the way up to high school. These schools have their stats for each level separated, hence there are multiple tabs of data.
Online Charter
Whether or not a school is an online school, a charter school, both, or neither.
